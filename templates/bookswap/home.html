Kzoo LitHub can help you save money

Book stores can be expensive. Trading websites have shipping charges when you buy, fees and commissions when you sell. As a campus, we already have many of the book that we need for the next quarter. If we can buy and sell to each other, we can save a lot of money on charges, fees and commissions.

How it works

When you find a book that you want to buy on Kzoo LitHub, you can contact the seller and meet on-campus to trade the book. If the book you are looking for is not available, you can subscribe to the book to receive notifications when that book is available.

Since the buyer and seller meet in person, there are no charges, fees or commissions from Kzoo LitHub.

About LitHub

The Kalamazoo College Computer Science Club develops and maintains the Kzoo LitHub website. LitHub is an open source project, licensed under the GNU GPLv3. You can modify and distribute the source code under the conditions of the license. Other colleges and communities are free to download the code from GitHub and run a LitHub of their own.

Features

  • Facebook integration: one click login (no username or password); register in under 20 seconds!
  • Google book preview: "look" at (and even inside) a book before you buy! (Available for many books)
  • NEW Subscribe to a book and be notified when copies of the book are listed for sale.
  • ISBN lookup: auto-populated book information based on the ISBN when you sell a book.
  • HTML5 speech recognition on search forms (currently only on Google Chrome)
  • Restricted to K college students
